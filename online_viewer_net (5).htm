Frequently asked questions

A proper diagnostic scan reads the live data from your vehicle's control modules — not just the fault code number, but the sensor readings, operating conditions and system parameters that help explain why a code was set. Guessing based on a code number alone often leads to replacing parts that weren't actually the problem. We use professional diagnostic equipment and interpret what we find, rather than just reading a code and ordering a part.

It depends on the fault. If the light is on steady and the car is driving normally, it's usually safe to drive a short distance to get it checked — but we wouldn't leave it long. If the light is flashing, if you can smell something unusual, or if the car is driving differently (misfiring, losing power, overheating), you should pull over and not drive it until it's been checked. When in doubt, give us a call and describe what you're experiencing — we can help you decide whether to drive in or arrange something else.

Yes, we work across all makes and models — Japanese, European, American and Australian vehicles. This includes passenger cars, SUVs, 4WDs and commercial/trade vehicles. Our diagnostic equipment covers a very broad range of manufacturers. If you're unsure whether we can help with your specific vehicle, just call us and we'll let you know.

For a straightforward fault with a clear code, an initial scan and assessment can take 30 to 60 minutes. More complex faults — especially intermittent electrical issues that don't always set a code — can take longer as we need to monitor live data and sometimes road test the vehicle under certain conditions to replicate the problem. We'll give you a realistic time estimate once we've had a first look at your vehicle.

Not necessarily. A flat battery can be caused by the battery itself failing, a faulty alternator that's not recharging it properly, or what's called a parasitic drain — something in the electrical system drawing current when the vehicle is switched off. We test all three. If you just replace the battery without checking the alternator and drain, you may find the new battery goes flat too. We check the whole charging system before recommending what needs to be replaced.

We can clear fault codes — but only after the underlying problem has been fixed. Clearing a code without fixing the fault just means the light will come back on, and it doesn't make the problem go away. If someone has offered to clear your warning light without diagnosing and fixing what caused it, that's not a solution. We fix the fault first, then clear the code and verify it doesn't return.
